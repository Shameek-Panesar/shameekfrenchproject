The Beast of Gevudan

In 1764, whispers filled the hills of Gévaudan, A terror emerged, a shadowy spawn. The Beast, they claimed, was a monstrous hound, With fiery eyes and a growling sound. It stalked the forests, fields, and streams, A nightmare born from villagers' dreams. With jaws that snapped and claws that tore, It claimed over a hundred, and still sought more. The king sent hunters, a knight, and gun, But the Beast evaded each and every one. A silver bullet, they finally decreed, Would end the curse, the monster's greed. Though the Beast fell, its truth unknown, Was it wolf, demon, or flesh and bone? In Gévaudan, its legend stays, A tale of fear from darker days. --- Let me know if you'd like to expand or adjust this!
