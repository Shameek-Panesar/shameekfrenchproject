Jeanne D'Arc

In Domrémy's quiet fields, a maiden stood tall, Her visions of saints were a clarion call. The Archangel spoke with a heavenly glow, "Save France, dear Jeanne, from her dire foe." A peasant in armor, with courage to spare, She donned the steel with a fervent prayer. At Orléans' gates, her banner she raised, Through battle and fire, her name was praised. "The Maid of Lorraine," her people would sing, With faith as her armor, she challenged the king. Yet treachery’s hand delivered her fate, To England's wrath, a fiery gate. But flames could not silence her voice so divine, Her spirit now soars, in France's shrine. A hero, a saint, a legend of lore, Joan of Arc lives forevermore.
